Computing

  • For LaTeX documents that are not both huge and written only by me, I usually use Overleaf, which is a sort of ‘Google Docs for LaTeX’. I think we should encourage students to use this instead of unweildy installations, and I will continue to think it’s great until one day the company goes bust and I lose all my work forever.
  • For R, my students convinced my I should be using RStudio, and they were extremely correct. I also like writing things in R Markdown; I consult Cosma Shalizi’s guidance or the big R Markdown book when I get stuck.
